📚 Grammar Rules You Practiced:

a / an: Use "a" before consonants (a cat), "an" before vowels (an apple)
is / are: "is" for singular (The cat IS), "are" for plural (The cats ARE)
Singular / Plural: Add -s or -es for plural nouns (1 dog → 2 dogs)
Possessives: his, her, my, their match the person (She → her, He → his)
Present Continuous: am/is/are + verb-ing (She IS wearing, not she WEARING)
